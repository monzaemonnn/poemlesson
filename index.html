About This Lesson

How English Really Sounds

Native English speakers don't say words one at a time. They group words into smooth phrases, blend sounds together, and stress the words that carry meaning. The rest gets swallowed, reduced, or linked.

We're going to use classic poems to practice these patterns. Poems are perfect because each line is short enough to repeat, and the rhythm is built right into the words.

🔲

Thought Groups

Words chunked into phrases with tiny pauses between them. This is the rhythm of English.

🔗

Linking Sounds

The end of one word flows into the next. Sometimes a new sound appears between them — like a ghost consonant.
